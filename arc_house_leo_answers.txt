=== REFERENCE: What "contract address you deployed" means ===
A smart contract is a program that lives on the blockchain. When you deploy it,
the chain gives it a permanent address (like a PO box). Anyone can visit that
address on ArcScan and see its code, every transaction, and every event it
emitted (e.g. "Concord paid X USDC").

The form asks for YOUR project's contract address so Circle's verification bot
can confirm you actually built something that runs on-chain — not just borrowed
Circle's infrastructure.

- INFRASTRUCTURE contracts (USDC token, CCTP bridge, the Circle agent wallet
  0x76bc...277a) belong to Circle. Do NOT list these — Tim's bot rejects them.
- YOUR contract = a program YOU deployed and control. For Concord that is the
  SubscriptionVault at 0xd25A1979a5bDa25c3ABd8b661957C2AaC9515a0F.

Using Circle tech (USDC, Circle Wallets/Contracts) to POWER the agent does NOT
make the vault "Circle's" — it's still Concord's code at Concord's address,
emitting Concord's Paid events. That's the proof the bot looks for.
